Tuna-Casserole

Description

Tuna Casserole is a comforting, budget-friendly dish made with cooked pasta, canned tuna, peas, and a creamy sauce (usually cream of mushroom soup or a homemade white sauce). Everything is mixed together, topped with shredded cheese and crispy breadcrumbs, then baked until golden and bubbly. It’s easy to make, filling, and perfect for a cozy dinner!

Ingredients

  • Pasta – 2 cups (uncooked elbow macaroni or egg noodles)
  • Canned tuna – 2 cans (drained, usually in water or oil)
  • Frozen peas – 1 cup (optional: can also use mixed vegetables)
  • Cream of mushroom soup – 1 can (about 10.5 oz / 300g)
  • Milk – ½ cup (to thin the soup slightly)
  • Shredded cheddar cheese – 1 to 1½ cups
  • Breadcrumbs – ½ cup (for topping)
  • Butter or olive oil – 1–2 tablespoons (for mixing with breadcrumbs or greasing the dish)
  • Salt and pepper – to taste

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 180°C (350°F) and cook 2 cups of egg noodles; drain and set aside.
  2. In a large bowl, mix 1 can tuna, 1 can cream of mushroom soup, 1/2 cup milk, 1 cup frozen peas, 1/2 cup cheese, seasonings, and the cooked noodles.
  3. Pour mixture into a greased baking dish.
  4. Mix 1/2 cup breadcrumbs with 1 tbsp melted butter, sprinkle on top with extra cheese.
  5. Bake for 25–30 minutes until golden and bubbly. Cool slightly and enjoy!
